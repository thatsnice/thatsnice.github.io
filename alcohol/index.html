From July 16th, 2015 to March 15th 2016, Robert de Forest was a non-drinker.

Events transpired which brought into stark contrast the differences between my model of reality and others'. After a particularly dark incident I made the decision to terminate my relationship with alcoholic beverages.

Why the past tense?

The rest of 2015 did not improve. Not long after the new year started I realized quitting had done little of what I had hoped it would. I lost a litle weight and spent less money, but my thinking didn't clear and my relationship with work had not improved. I also learned that my relationships with my best friend and my wife had not deteriorated significantly when I stopped, they were just concerned for me. I identified that work and psych med changes had been far greater contributors to my frustrations than alcohol had. I decided then that I would start drinking again, but only after I had put measures in place to better protect me from those aspects of drinking which I still feared.

And what measures are those?

I will only drink so long as I can ensure I continue to evaluate my drinking against my requirements. My current (March 2016) plan is to write a tweet-sized note in Trello for every three drinks or "drink equivalent" portions I consume. The note should describe either what about the drink or circumstances I am enjoying.

So you have some way of ensuring that now?

Not exactly. I am working on a tool which I will use to remind me to take notes while I'm "partying". Eventually the tool will be smart enough to notice when I've taken a note and only remind me if I haven't taken one in over an hour.

But the tool is not yet done.

So .. something happened?

On March 14th, 2016 I had a minor accident on my motorcycle (I'm fine). I had little more than bruising, but it the pain was rather annoying on the 15th. That night I decided that since I had a plan, and since the next day was my birthday that as long as I remembered to take notes I wasn't cheating and I could split a bottle of wine with Raine while watching Poirot on Netflix, and I did.

It was devine.
