The Short Version

  • My drinking escalated gradually over the course of about 20 years.
  • On the night of 2015-07-15 all three spheres of my life seemed to be threatened by things I had said or done under the influence.
  • At about 2am I was starting to have intense anxiety and was thinking about suicide.
  • In 2+ years of therapy I had learned to monitor my thoughts and when I noticed myself thinking these things I checked myself in to the ER.
  • I was at the hospital for 3 hours, most of which was a conversation with a social worker. I developed a more realistic assessment of my lifestyle and its impact on the things I care about. I understood my options.
  • I spent the first half of Thursday mulling and brooding.
  • I decided to quit drinking. Details of this decisions appear under  How I Decided To Stop.
  • I talked with Raine about what had happened Wednesday and she helped me work on a detox plan based on drinking carefully metered and decreasing amounts of alcohol over the course of five days to minimize the risks associated with quitting suddenly.  See the HAMS article How To Taper Off Alcoholif you're interested in the process.
  • Friday I met with my psychiatrist. She prescribed me lorazepam to use for the detox process in lieu of alcohol. This simplified the process dramatically because lorazepam lasts for 3-4 hours and has no intoxicating effect in the doses she prescribed.
  • I had my last dose of lorazepam the next Monday. As of this writing that was three days ago and I feel fine.
